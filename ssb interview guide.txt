SSB Interview Preparation Guide

Purpose
This guide provides a complete, step-by-step plan to prepare for the Services Selection Board (SSB) interview. It focuses on developing officer-like qualities, improving communication, and performing confidently across all testing stages.

Overview of SSB Stages
1. Screening (Day 1)
   - OIR (verbal and non-verbal reasoning)
   - PPDT (Picture Perception and Discussion Test)
2. Psychological Tests (Day 2)
   - TAT (Thematic Apperception Test)
   - WAT (Word Association Test)
   - SRT (Situation Reaction Test)
   - SDT (Self-Description Test)
3. GTO Tasks (Day 3–4)
   - Group discussions, group planning
   - PGT/CT/IRT (group tasks)
   - Command task
   - Individual obstacles
   - Lecturette
   - Final group task
4. Interview (Day 2–4, individual slot)
5. Conference (Final day)

Preparation Plan (8 Weeks)

Week 1: Baseline and Foundation
- Understand SSB process and OLQs
- Take a diagnostic mock for OIR, PPDT, and interview
- Start a daily routine:
  - 20 minutes reasoning practice
  - 20 minutes reading (newspaper/editorials)
  - 10 minutes spoken summary
- Begin fitness baseline: run 1.5 km, basic push-ups/squats

Week 2: OIR and Communication
- OIR practice daily: 30 minutes (mixed verbal/non-verbal)
- Learn PPDT structure and practice 1 story per day
- Practice group discussion basics: clarity, brevity, relevance
- Improve vocabulary and comprehension through editorials

Week 3: Psychological Tests (TAT/WAT/SRT)
- TAT: 2 pictures per day, 4-minute story drills
- WAT: 60 words in 15 minutes; focus on positive, realistic reactions
- SRT: 30 situations daily; keep responses action-oriented
- Start SDT draft using feedback from family/friends

Week 4: Interview Readiness
- Prepare personal background notes:
  - Family, education, achievements, failures, hobbies
  - Strengths, weaknesses, life goals
- Conduct 2 mock interviews this week
- Practice clear, concise answers (STAR format)

Week 5: GTO Orientation
- Learn GTO tasks and rules
- Practice group dynamics: cooperation, initiative, helping others
- Improve physical stamina:
  - 2–3 km run
  - basic obstacle drills if possible
- Begin lecturette practice: 2 topics per day

Week 6: Full Mock + Refinement
- Take a full mock (screening + psych + interview style Qs)
- Analyze mistakes and improve weak areas
- Continue daily TAT/WAT/SRT practice
- Refine SDT into final version

Week 7: Personality Alignment
- Focus on OLQs: leadership, responsibility, social adaptability
- Practice real-life application: volunteer, team tasks, sports
- Daily reflection journal: what you did well and what to improve

Week 8: Final Polishing
- Reduce volume; increase quality
- Short mock of each test type
- Revise interview notes
- Maintain fitness and confidence
- Sleep, diet, and stress control

Daily Routine (Minimum)
- 30 minutes reasoning
- 30 minutes psych practice (rotating TAT/WAT/SRT)
- 20 minutes reading and 10-minute spoken summary
- 20–30 minutes physical training
- 5-minute reflection journal

Key Tips for Success
- Be natural and honest; avoid scripted behavior
- Focus on team contribution in group tasks
- Develop clear, positive, and realistic responses
- Maintain confidence and steady communication
- Consistency beats intensity

Quick Checklist Before Reporting
- Documents prepared and verified
- Formal dress ready (interview and GTO)
- Travel and reporting details confirmed
- Adequate rest and hydration

End of Guide
